Tiger

Tigers are the largest cat species in the world and the third-largest carnivore on land. Only polar and brown bears are larger.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera Tigris
  • Average Weight: Male: 200-680 lbs, Female: 140-370 lbs
  • Average Lifespan: 16-18 years
  • Habitat: Rain Forests, Grasslands, Savannas and even Mangrove Swamps

The largest of the big cats, tigers are truly impressive animals with their black-striped orange fur and white bellies. It is an apex predator, primarily preying on ungulates such as deer and wild boar.
